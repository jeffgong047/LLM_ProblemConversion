Example of non-uniqueness[edit]
To understand uniqueness of solutions, consider the following examples.[3] A differential equation can possess a stationary point. For example, for the equation dy/dt = ay (a<0<0), the stationary solution is y(t) = 0, which is obtained for the initial condition y(0) = 0. Beginning with another initial condition y(0) = y0 â‰  0, the solution y(t) tends toward the stationary point, but reaches it only at the limit of infinite time, so the uniqueness of solutions (over all finite times) is guaranteed.

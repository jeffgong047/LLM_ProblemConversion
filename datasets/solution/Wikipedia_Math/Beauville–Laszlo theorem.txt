Global version[edit]
In geometric language, the Beauvilleâ€“Laszlo theorem allows one to glue sheaves on a one-dimensional affine scheme over an infinitesimal neighborhood of a point.  Since sheaves have a "local character" and since any scheme is locally affine, the theorem admits a global statement of the same nature.  The version of this statement that the authors found noteworthy concerns vector bundles:

Mathematical identity involving sums of binomial coefficients
Abel's binomial theorem, named after Niels Henrik Abel, is a mathematical identity involving sums of binomial coefficients. It states the following:

∑k=0m(mk)(w+m−k)m−k−1(z+k)k=w−1(z+w+m)m.∑_k=0^mmk(w+m-k)^m-k-1(z+k)^k=w^-1(z+w+m)^m.

Example[edit]
The case m = 2[edit]
(20)(w+2)1(z+0)0+(21)(w+1)0(z+1)1+(22)(w+0)−1(z+2)2=(w+2)+2(z+1)+(z+2)2w=(z+w+2)2w.     20(w+2)^1(z+0)^0+21(w+1)^0(z+1)^1+22(w+0)^-1(z+2)^2
   =(w+2)+2(z+1)+(z+2)^2/w
   =(z+w+2)^2/w.

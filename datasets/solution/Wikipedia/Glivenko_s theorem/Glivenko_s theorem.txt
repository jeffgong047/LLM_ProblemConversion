In probability theory, Glivenko's theorem states that if φn,n∈Nφ_n,n∈ℕ, φφ are the characteristic functions of some probability distributions μn,μμ_n,μ respectively and φn→φφ_n→φ almost everywhere, then μn→μμ_n→μ in the sense of probability distributions.[1]

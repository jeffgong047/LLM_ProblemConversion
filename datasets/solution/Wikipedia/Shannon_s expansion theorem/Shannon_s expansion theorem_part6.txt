History: George Boole presented this expansion as his Proposition II, "To expand or develop a function involving any number of logical symbols", in his Laws of Thought (1854),[3] and it was "widely applied by Boole and other nineteenth-century logicians".[4] Claude Shannon mentioned this expansion, among other Boolean identities, in a 1949 paper,[5] and showed the switching network interpretations of the identity.

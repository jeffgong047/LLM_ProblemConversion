Please help improve this article by introducing citations to additional sources.Find sources: "Five color theorem" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR (July 2023) A Five-Color Map The five color theorem is a result from graph theory that given a plane separated into regions, such as a political map of the countries of the world, the regions may be colored using no more than five colors in such a way that no two adjacent regions receive the same color. The five color theorem is implied by the stronger four color theorem, but is considerably easier to prove. It was based on a failed attempt at the four color proof by Alfred Kempe in 1879.

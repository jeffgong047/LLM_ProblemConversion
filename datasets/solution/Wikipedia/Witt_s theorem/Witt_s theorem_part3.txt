theorem: Let (V, q), (V1, q1), (V2, q2) be three quadratic spaces over a field k. Assume that (V1,q1)⊕(V,q)≃(V2,q2)⊕(V,q).(V_1,q_1)⊕(V,q)≃(V_2,q_2)⊕(V,q). Then the quadratic spaces (V1, q1) and (V2, q2) are isometric: (V1,q1)≃(V2,q2).(V_1,q_1)≃(V_2,q_2). In other words, the direct summand (V, q) appearing in both sides of an isomorphism between quadratic spaces may be "cancelled". Witt's decomposition

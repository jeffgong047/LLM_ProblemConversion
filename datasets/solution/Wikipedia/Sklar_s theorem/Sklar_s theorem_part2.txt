definition: Consider a random vector (X1,X2,…,Xd)(X_1,X_2,…,X_d). Suppose its marginals are continuous, i.e. the marginal CDFs Fi(x)=Pr[Xi≤x]_i(x)=[X_i] are continuous functions. By applying the probability integral transform to each component, the random vector (U1,U2,…,Ud)=(F1(X1),F2(X2),…,Fd(Xd))(U_1,U_2,…,U_d)=(F_1(X_1),F_2(X_2),…,F_d(X_d)) has marginals that are uniformly distributed on the interval [0, 1]. The copula of (X1,X2,…,Xd)(X_1,X_2,…,X_d) is defined as the joint cumulative distribution function of (U1,U2,…,Ud)(U_1,U_2,…,U_d): C(u1,u2,…,ud)=Pr[U1≤u1,U2≤u2,…,Ud≤ud].(u_1,u_2,…,u_d)=[U_1_1,U_2_2,…,U_d_d]. The copula C contains all information on the dependence structure between the components of (X1,X2,…,Xd)(X_1,X_2,…,X_d) whereas the marginal cumulative distribution functions Fi_i contain all information on the marginal distributions of Xi_i. The reverse of these steps can be used to generate pseudo-random samples from general classes of multivariate probability distributions. That is, given a procedure to generate a sample (U1,U2,…,Ud)(U_1,U_2,…,U_d) from the copula function, the required sample can be constructed as (X1,X2,…,Xd)=(F1−1(U1),F2−1(U2),…,Fd−1(Ud)).(X_1,X_2,…,X_d)=(F_1^-1(U_1),F_2^-1(U_2),…,F_d^-1(U_d)). The generalized inverses Fi−1_i^-1 are unproblematic almost surely, since the Fi_i were assumed to be continuous. Furthermore, the above formula for the copula function can be rewritten as: C(u1,u2,…,ud)=Pr[X1≤F1−1(u1),X2≤F2−1(u2),…,Xd≤Fd−1(ud)].(u_1,u_2,…,u_d)=[X_1_1^-1(u_1),X_2_2^-1(u_2),…,X_d_d^-1(u_d)].

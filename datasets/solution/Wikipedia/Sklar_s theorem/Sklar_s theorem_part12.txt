copulas: When studying multivariate data, one might want to investigate the underlying copula. Suppose we have observations (X1i,X2i,…,Xdi),i=1,…,n(X_1^i,X_2^i,…,X_d^i), i=1,…,n from a random vector (X1,X2,…,Xd)(X_1,X_2,…,X_d) with continuous marginals. The corresponding “true” copula observations would be (U1i,U2i,…,Udi)=(F1(X1i),F2(X2i),…,Fd(Xdi)),i=1,…,n.(U_1^i,U_2^i,…,U_d^i)=(F_1(X_1^i),F_2(X_2^i),…,F_d(X_d^i)), i=1,…,n. However, the marginal distribution functions Fi_i are usually not known. Therefore, one can construct pseudo copula observations by using the empirical distribution functions Fkn(x)=1n∑i=1n1(Xki≤x)_k^n(x)=1/n∑_i=1^n1(X_k^i) instead. Then, the pseudo copula observations are defined as (U 1i,U 2i,…,U di)=(F1n(X1i),F2n(X2i),…,Fdn(Xdi)),i=1,…,n.(Ũ_1^i,Ũ_2^i,…,Ũ_d^i)=(F_1^n(X_1^i),F_2^n(X_2^i),…,F_d^n(X_d^i)), i=1,…,n. The corresponding empirical copula is then defined as Cn(u1,…,ud)=1n∑i=1n1(U 1i≤u1,…,U di≤ud).^n(u_1,…,u_d)=1/n∑_i=1^n1(Ũ_1^i_1,…,Ũ_d^i_d). The components of the pseudo copula samples can also be written as U ki=Rki/nŨ_k^i=R_k^i/n, where Rki_k^i is the rank of the observation Xki_k^i: Rki=∑j=1n1(Xkj≤Xki)_k^i=∑_j=1^n1(X_k^j_k^i) Therefore, the empirical copula can be seen as the empirical distribution of the rank transformed data.

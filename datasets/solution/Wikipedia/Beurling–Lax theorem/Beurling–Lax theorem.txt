In mathematics, the  Beurling–Lax theorem is a theorem due to Beurling (1949) and Lax (1959) which characterizes the shift-invariant subspaces of the Hardy space H2(D,C)^2(𝔻,ℂ). It states that each such space is of the form

θH2(D,C),^2(𝔻,ℂ),
for some inner function θθ.

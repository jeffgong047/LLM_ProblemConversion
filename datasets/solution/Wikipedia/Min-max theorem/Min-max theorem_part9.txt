Sometimes we have some eigenvalues below the essential spectrum, and we would like to approximate the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions. Theorem (Min-Max). Let A be self-adjoint, and let E1≤E2≤E3≤⋯_1_2_3≤⋯ be the eigenvalues of A below the essential spectrum. Then En=minψ1,…,ψnmax⟨ψ,Aψ⟩:ψ∈span⁡(ψ1,…,ψn),‖ψ‖=1_n=min_ψ_1,…,ψ_nmax{⟨ψ,Aψ⟩:ψ∈span(ψ_1,…,ψ_n), ψ=1}. If we only have N eigenvalues and hence run out of eigenvalues, then we let En:=infσess(A)_n:=infσ_ess(A) (the bottom of the essential spectrum) for n>N, and the above statement holds after replacing min-max with inf-sup. Theorem (Max-Min). Let A be self-adjoint, and let E1≤E2≤E3≤⋯_1_2_3≤⋯ be the eigenvalues of A below the essential spectrum. Then En=maxψ1,…,ψn−1min⟨ψ,Aψ⟩:ψ⊥ψ1,…,ψn−1,‖ψ‖=1_n=max_ψ_1,…,ψ_n-1min{⟨ψ,Aψ⟩:ψ⊥ψ_1,…,ψ_n-1, ψ=1}. If we only have N eigenvalues and hence run out of eigenvalues, then we let En:=infσess(A)_n:=infσ_ess(A) (the bottom of the essential spectrum) for n > N, and the above statement holds after replacing max-min with sup-inf.

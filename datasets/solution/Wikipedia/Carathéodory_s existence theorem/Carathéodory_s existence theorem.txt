In mathematics, CarathÃ©odory's existence theorem says that an ordinary differential equation has a solution under relatively mild conditions. It is a generalization of Peano's existence theorem. Peano's theorem requires that the right-hand side of the differential equation be continuous, while CarathÃ©odory's theorem shows existence of solutions (in a more general sense) for some discontinuous equations. The theorem is named after Constantin CarathÃ©odory.
